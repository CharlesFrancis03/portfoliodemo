about me

I'm Mark Zuckerberg, as the co-founder and CEO of Facebook, has left an indelible mark on the world of technology and social media. Facebook, launched in 2004, began as a platform for Harvard students and swiftly expanded globally under his guidance, revolutionizing the way people connect online. It introduced the concept of social profiles, enabling users to share personal information, photos, and updates with friends and family, becoming a primary mode of communication across the globe. Zuckerberg's leadership transformed Facebook into a thriving business through monetization and strategic acquisitions of companies like Instagram and WhatsApp. Facebook's influence extends to digital marketing, with businesses leveraging its power for targeted advertising. Beyond its economic impact, Facebook has shaped society, politics, and activism by organizing and mobilizing movements, while also sparking debates on issues like fake news and privacy. In 2021, Zuckerberg rebranded the company as "Meta Platforms, Inc.," signaling his ambitious vision for the metaverse and the future of technology. His role in shaping digital communication and the tech industry remains profound and ever-evolving.


projects

Facebook (formerly The Facebook)

Mark Zuckerberg, along with his college roommates Andrew McCollum, Eduardo Saverin, Chris Hughes, and Dustin Moskovitz, created Facebook in 2004 while he was a student at Harvard University. Facebook quickly became one of the most popular social networking platforms in the world, connecting billions of people and changing the way people communicate and share information.

Open Compute Project (OCP)

Zuckerberg initiated the Open Compute Project in 2011. OCP is an open-source hardware design initiative that focuses on creating efficient and sustainable data center infrastructure. It encourages the sharing of hardware designs and innovations among tech companies.

Meta Platforms, Inc.

In 2021, Facebook was rebranded as Meta Platforms, Inc. Mark Zuckerberg announced this new corporate identity to reflect the company's expanding focus on the metaverse, virtual and augmented reality, and the development of the "metaverse" concept.

Internet.org (now Free Basics)

Zuckerberg launched Internet.org in 2013, with the goal of making internet access more affordable and accessible in developing countries. This project aimed to connect people in underserved areas to the internet by providing free access to a limited set of online services.
